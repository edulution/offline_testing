Mr and Mrs Chipinde were' poor farmers because their farm was very small. They grew vegetables and groundnuts, which they sold at the village market. They wanted their son to go to school because they wanted him to get a job in town. “We will send Mwepu to school,” Mr Chipinde said. “But Kafusha will not be sent to school because she will be helping me with housework,” said Mrs Chipinde. Mwepu and Kafusha liked talking to each other. Sometimes he taught her things he was learning at school. He asked her some questions. She answered most of them correctly. Mwepu was happy because his sister was a very clever girl.
