READING COMPREHENSION

Christmas Day, the 25th of December, is the biggest annual festival which is celebrated in Zambia and many other parts of the world. Although this festival has religious origins, not everyone celebrates it as the birth of Jesus.

In large shops, preparations for Christmas begin six weeks before the actual day. Shop owners like Christmas because lots of people buy gifts, which means more goods are bought from the shops than at any other time in the year.

Although many people think it is far too early, the main shops are decorated five or six weeks before. This is done to attract people to buy things. In larger towns, decorations are put up in the main streets two or three weeks before Christmas. Usually these decorations are lit up at night, which looks attractive. Since preparations are displayed so early, some people say they are bored with Christmas before the actual day.

It is especially busy on Christmas Eve. Preparations are also made in homes. Special puddings and cakes are eaten on Christmas Day and these are often cooked in advance. Many people do not bother and leave their cooking until the last minute. Houses are also decorated to make them more attractive.

Christmas Day is particularly enjoyed by children who get very excited about the special foods and gifts. The day after Christmas is called Boxing Day and it is also a holiday in some parts of the world. On Boxing Day, some people rest after the celebrations of the previous day.
